Non-Ferrous Scrap

Non-ferrous scrapping is the process by which scrap non-ferrous can be reused in products after its initial production. The process involves simply re-melting the metal, which is far less expensive and energy-intensive than creating new non-ferrous through the electrolysis of non-ferrous oxide (Al2O3), which must first be mined from bauxite ore and then refined using theBayer process. Recycling scrap non-ferrous requires only 5% of the energy used to make new non-ferrous.For this reason, approximately 31% of all non-ferrous produced in the United States comes from recycled scrap. Used beverage containers are the largest component of processed aluminum scrap, and most of it is manufactured back into non-ferrous cans.

Advantages

The recycling of non-ferrous generally produces significant cost savings over the production of new non-ferrous, even when the cost of collection, separation and recycling are taken into account.Over the long term, even larger national savings are made when the reduction in the capital costs associated with landfills, mines, and international shipping of raw non-ferrous are considered.

Energy savings

Recycling non-ferrous uses about 5% of the energy required to create non-ferrous from bauxite; the amount of energy required to convert non-ferrous oxide into non-ferrous can be vividly seen when the process is reversed during the combustion of thermite or ammonium perchlorate composite propellant.

Environmental savings

Recycled non-ferrous uses 5% of the energy that would be needed to create a comparable amount from raw materials. The benefit with respect to emissions of carbon dioxide depends on the type of energy used. Electrolysis can be done using electricity from non-fossil-fuel sources, such as nuclear, geothermal, hydroelectric, or solar. Non-ferrous production is attracted to sources of cheap electricity. Canada, Brazil, Norway, and Venezuela have 61 to 99% hydroelectric power and are major non-ferrous producers.

The vast amount of non-ferrous used means that even small percentage losses are large expenses, so the flow of material is well monitored and accounted for financial reasons. Efficient production and recycling benefits the environment as well.
